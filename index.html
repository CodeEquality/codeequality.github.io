About

What is Code Equality?

Code Equality is a non-profit initiative that aims to promote computer science among Malaysians, with the hope that those who gain early exposure would go on to pursue a career in tech.

Our goals are two-fold:

  • Making computer science education more accessible to all Malaysians.
  • Encouraging more women participation in computer science.

We believe in equal opportunities in learning computer science, hence our name -- Code Equality. As such, we strive to ensure our events are available to all.

Who are we?

We're currently a team of two co-founders.

Wunmin Wong Wunmin graduated from MIT in Management Science with minors in Energy and Economics. Her biggest regret is not majoring in Computer Science in university, hence she’s now turning that missed opportunity into a motivation to prevent other young Malaysians from having the same regret.

Sher Minn Chong Sher Minn graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a degree in Computer Science. She is a consultant at IBM Malaysia during the day and a freelance web developer and designer by night. She's always excited about teaching programming, web development and design, hand-brewed coffee and bad puns. You can find her on Twitter most days.

How did we start?

Long story short: it was difficult talking to organizations and schools as two random people who want to offer programming courses. It made sense to build an organization to represent our objectives. So here we are.

Get involved

Like what we do? Have an idea for an event?

If you're interested in getting involved with Code Equality, there are many ways you can help!

Teach or talk.

Regardless of whether you’re a coding ninja or a coding newb, if you have the enthusiasm to help others learn coding, teach a bootcamp with us or be a teaching assistant!

Host an event.

Invite us to your school or organization to hold a talk or bootcamp. We provide the content; you provide the venue.

Pitch us an idea!

Anything else you’d love us to organize? Let us know!
